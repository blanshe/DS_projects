Birth, Fertility Rate & Babies of Unmarried Mothers

I used data from Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to visualize birth rate, fertility rate and babies born to unmarried mothers.

By MUSINGUZI BLANSHE

Published August 19, 2019

First, I looked at birth rate of races between 1980 and 2015. I got the data set from National Vital Statistics Reports, Volume 66, Number 1, January 5, 2017. You can find it on page 16. You can find the data set here

The birth rate has been falling among all race groups, but far more among American-Indian-Alaska natives. They had the highest rate in 1980 but by 2015, they had the lowest birth rate.

Fertility Rate

From the same data set, I looked at fertility rate. Once again, fertility rate has been on the downward trend but far more among American-Indian-Alaska natives than other race groups.
