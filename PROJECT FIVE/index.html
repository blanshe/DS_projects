Unemployment in U.S States

In this project I examine U.S states that registered highest and lowest unemployment rates in the first half of 2019. I went ahead to graph nonfarm labor productivity among U.S states.

By Musinguzi Blanshe

Published August 11th, 2019

I downloaded data set of U.S states unemployment in the first half of 2019 from the U.S Bureau of Labor Statistics. I sorted data and picked states that had highest and lowest unemployment from January to June.

Highest unemployment: Alaska, District of Columbia, West Virginia, New Mexico and Arizona were the states with highest unemployment rates. But West Virginia stood out. Why? It registered a significant unemployment rate reduction from 5.2 to 4.4 percent.

States with high unemployment

West Virginia stood out as its unemployment rate went down significantly.

Data source: U.S Bureau of Labor Statistics

Low unemployment

I then turned to states with low unemployment rate. Hawaii, North Dakota, Vermont, New Hampshire, Iowa were the states with lowest unemployment rate. Vermont stood out in this cluster because its unemployment rate went down from 2.5 to 2.1 percent.

Unemployment in Iowa was static for the first half of 2019. The rate went up in Hawaii from February to June. The rate went up in New Hampshire in June. Unemployment went down in North Dakota in January and February but remained static during the four months that followed.

Vermont stood out

Vermont registered a significant unemployment reduction in this cluster.

Data source: U.S Bureau of Labor Statistics

labor productivity

I further analyzed 2017-16 nonfarm labor productivity in U.S states. I also picked the data set from U.S Bureau of Labor Statistics. Montana had the highest productivity and Arkansas the lowest.

Labor productivity across states

Montana had the highest labor productivity of 2.0 percent.

Data source: U.S Bureau of Labor Statistics
